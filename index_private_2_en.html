Bankruptcy of private individuals

Given the unstable economic situation in the country, one of the ways for an ordinary person to get out of a difficult financial situation is to initiate the procedure for declaring an individual insolvent.

Learn more

The Bankruptcy Code has introduced a new mechanism for repayment by an individual of a foreign currency loan secured by a mortgage on an apartment or a residential building that is the sole residence of the debtor's family - restructuring of an individual's debt on a foreign currency loan with a maturity of 10 to 15 years. In this case, a strong argument in favor of the procedure for declaring an individual insolvent is that the debt is fixed in the national currency - hryvnia as of the date of opening insolvency proceedings, the amount of debt does not include penalties and fines, and the claims are repaid in the amount of the market value of the apartment or residential house (as of the current date) minus the amount repaid on the loan principal before the opening of insolvency proceedings.

In other words, regardless of the amount received under a foreign currency loan, the amount to be restructured (within 10 or 15 years) in an individual's insolvency case is the amount equal to the market value of the mortgaged apartment or house, which is the only home of the debtor and his family, reduced in proportion to the part of the loan repaid by the debtor before the insolvency proceedings were opened.

The above new instrument allows an individual to repay a debt to a financial institution for 10-15 years in the national currency, hryvnia, based on the market value of the mortgaged property.

What is bankruptcy of an individual?

  • The inability of an individual to fulfill his or her obligations (repay debts) to creditors in a timely manner and in full (insolvency).
    • In other words, this is a legal procedure that allows you to get out of a debt hole, start your financial life over, from a clean slate, and get rid of arrests, mortgages, and other restrictions caused by debts.

For what you need to initiate bankruptcy proceedings?

  • This is an opportunity for each debtor to repay debts to creditors in the amount in accordance with their financial opportunities or to write off (repay) debts.

Who is it for?

  • Any private individual.

What result can be expected? (What is the point of this procedure?)

  • After a court opens an insolvency case against an individual, the insolvency proceedings are suspended:

    - Fulfillment of obligations by the debtor, including taxes and fees;

    - Recovery from the debtor of an individual under all enforcement documents (except for alimony, compensation for damage caused by injury, other damage to health or death of an individual, as well as except for cases when enforcement proceedings are at the stage of distribution of monetary amounts recovered from the debtor, including those received from the sale of the debtor's property, or when the property is at the stage of sale from the moment the information on the sale is made public);

    - No forfeit (fine, penalty) or other financial sanctions are imposed for non-fulfillment or improper fulfillment of obligations to satisfy the claims covered by the moratorium;

    - The limitation period for claims against the debtor;

    - The inflation index for the entire period of delay in fulfillment of the debtor's monetary obligations is not applied.
